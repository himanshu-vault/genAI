India got its freedom on 15th August 1947 after a very long and painfull strugle against the British rule. For almost 200 years, the British controlled India and used its resourses for their own benifit. The people of India were not happy and they slowly started raising there voice against the injustice.

The freedom movement was not easy and many brave leaders sacrified there lifes for the country. Mahatma Gandhi was one of the most important leaders who belived in non-violance and truth. He started many movments like Non-Cooperation Movement and Quit India Movement which united lakhs of Indians. Other leaders like Jawaharlal Nehru, Subhas Chandra Bose and Bhagat Singh also played a very important role in the fight for indepndence.

Before 1947, Indians had to follow many unfair laws and pay heavy taxes. They did not have freedom of speech and were treated unequally in there own land. The Revolt of 1857 was one of the first major uprisings which showed that Indians wanted to be free. Later, the Indian National Congress became an important platform to demand rights and self rule.

Finally, after many years of protests, jail punishments and sacrifises, India achived freedom. But independence also came with the partition of India and Pakistan, which caused a lot of violance and suffering among the people.

Today we celebrate Independence Day every year with great proud and respect. The national flag is hoisted and people remember the heros who fought bravly for the nation. Freedom is not just about being free from foreign rule, but also about having rights and responsiblities. It is our duty to respect our country and work hard for its developement.
