After nearly 200 years of British rule, India finally gained independence on **15 August 1947**. This historic moment was the result of continuous struggle, determination, and leadership by countless freedom fighters.

The British East India Company first came to India for trade in the 17th century. Over time, they gained political control and established British rule. The people of India suffered under unfair laws, heavy taxes, and economic exploitation. Gradually, Indians began to unite and raise their voices against foreign rule.

One of the earliest major revolts was the Revolt of 1857, often called the First War of Independence. Although it failed, it awakened a strong sense of nationalism among Indians. Later, the formation of the Indian National Congress in 1885 gave a structured platform to demand rights and reforms.

Many great leaders played important roles in the freedom movement. Mahatma Gandhi introduced the principles of non-violence and civil disobedience. Movements like the Non-Cooperation Movement and Quit India Movement united millions of Indians. Leaders such as Jawaharlal Nehru, Subhas Chandra Bose, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, and Bhagat Singh also made great sacrifices for the nation.

After years of struggle, negotiations, and sacrifices, India finally achieved independence in 1947. However, independence also came with the partition of India and Pakistan, which caused great pain and migration for millions of people.

Today, India celebrates Independence Day every year with pride and patriotism. The Prime Minister hoists the national flag at the Red Fort in Delhi and addresses the nation. This day reminds us of the sacrifices made by our freedom fighters and our responsibility to protect and strengthen our democracy.

Freedom not only means being free from foreign rule, but also having the right to speak, think, and live with dignity. As citizens of India, it is our duty to respect our country, follow the law, and work towards its progress.
